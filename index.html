Dinosaurs were a diverse group of reptiles that dominated the Earth for over 160 million years, from the Triassic period through the end of the Cretaceous. They evolved into countless shapes and sizes, ranging from small, bird-like hunters to towering long-necked giants. Their success came from their adaptability, unique skeletal structures, and the wide range of environments they inhabited.




One of the most fascinating aspects of dinosaurs is how different they were from one another. Some, like the massive sauropods, walked on four pillar-like legs and fed on vegetation high in the treetops. Others, such as swift theropods, were agile carnivores with sharp teeth and claws. Many species likely had feathers, vibrant colors, and complex social behaviors, showing that dinosaurs were far more dynamic than once believed.
